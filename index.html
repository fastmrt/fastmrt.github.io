FastMRT

FastMRT is a collaborative research project that aims to improve the speed and quality of MRI scans for thermometery. Our goal is to make MRI scans up faster while maintaining or even improving image quality.

About FastMRT

FastMRT is a collaborative research project that aims to improve the speed and quality of MRI scans for thermometery.

Our team of researchers and engineers are working on developing new machine learning algorithms to achieve this goal. We are also working with clinical partners to validate our methods and ensure that they are safe and effective for patients.

If you are interested in learning more about our research or collaborating with us, please contact us at fastmr.thermometry@gmail.com.

Our Research

At FastMRT, we are working on developing new machine learning algorithms and hardware solutions to improve the speed and quality of MRI scans for thermometery. Our research focuses on three main areas:

  • Image Reconstruction: We are developing new algorithms to reconstruct high-quality images from undersampled MRI data. By reducing the amount of data needed for each scan, we can make MRI scans faster without sacrificing image quality.
  • Hardware Acceleration: We are exploring new hardware solutions to accelerate MRI scans. By optimizing the hardware used in MRI machines, we can reduce scan times and improve patient throughput.
  • Validation and Clinical Translation: We are working with clinical partners to validate our methods and ensure that they are safe and effective for patients. We are also exploring ways to integrate our methods into clinical workflows to improve patient outcomes.

If you are interested in learning more about our research or collaborating with us, please contact us at fastmrt@fb.com.

Get Involved

There are many ways to get involved with FastMRT:

  • Download the Dataset: The FastMRT dataset is available for download on our Datasets page. Use it to develop your own machine learning algorithms and test them against state-of-the-art methods.
  • Participate in the Challenge: We host an annual FastMRT Challenge to encourage researchers to develop new algorithms for MRI reconstruction. Visit our Challenge page to learn more.
  • Collaborate with Us: We are always looking for new collaborators to help us advance the field of MRI reconstruction. If you are interested in working with us, please contact us at fastmrt@fb.com.
